MMA report: A Perfect Storm

In October 2025, the Massachusetts Municipal Association (MMA) released a report that examines the key factors pressuring municipal budgets.

Massachusetts municipalities provide essential services - from schools and public safety to roads and clean water - but their budgets are being squeezed by a structural mismatch between rising costs and constrained revenue options. Local income and broad sales taxes are prohibited, and Proposition 2 1/2 generally caps annual property tax levy growth at 2.5 percent (plus new growth), leaving communities with limited ability to keep pace with inflation.

State aid remains important but is less generous than the national average and has become less helpful for core operating needs because Unrestricted General Government Aid (UGGA) has fallen sharply in real terms - down about 25 percent since 2002 across community types. Temporary federal pandemic relief has also ended, removing a key short-term buffer.

While pressure is widespread, impacts vary: rural towns and many suburbs have seen real declines in state aid and have struggled to sustain education and staffing, while gateway cities have received more targeted education aid but lag in other spending areas. Overrides can exceed the tax cap, but they require referenda and are unevenly feasible. Overall, the report portrays a statewide fiscal "vice" that calls for both flexible state support and solutions tailored to different local conditions.
