Brookline's FY27-FY31 Long Range Financial Forecast

The FY27-FY Long Range Financial Plan was presented to the Advisory Committee on January 13, 2026.

The Brookline FY2027-FY2031 Long-Range Financial Plan, presented in January 2026, outlines a challenging fiscal outlook characterized by a significant structural deficit and the expiration of federal pandemic-era funding.

Financial Outlook and Deficits

The town faces an immediate and growing gap between projected revenues and the costs required to maintain current service levels.

  • Total Structural Deficit: For FY2027, the town and schools face a combined deficit of approximately $16.6 million (which grows cumulatively to over $42 million by FY2031).
  • School Deficit: The Public Schools of Brookline (PSB) account for the largest portion of the gap, with a projected $13.5 million structural deficit in FY2027.
  • Town Deficit: Municipal departments face a $2.9 million structural deficit in FY2027.

Key Cost Drivers

  • Personnel Benefits: Employee benefits are the single largest driver of expenditure growth. Health insurance rates are projected to increase by 12% in FY2027, with annual 10% increases thereafter.
  • ARPA Expiration: Federal American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA) funding expires in December 2026. While the town has prepared for this, the loss of these funds creates pressure to find new revenue for popular programs like Senior Medical Transit.
  • Inflation and Collective Bargaining: Ongoing pressures from inflation and necessary funding for police, fire, and school labor contracts continue to outpace traditional revenue growth.

Revenue Constraints

  • Proposition 2 1/2: The town's primary revenue source, property taxes, remains limited by state law to a 2.5% annual increase plus "new growth," which is not keeping pace with a projected 7-8% increase in expenditures.
  • Stagnant Aid: State aid (UGGA and Chapter 70) is projected to grow modestly (1%-2.5%), offering little relief for the structural gap.
  • Local Receipts: Sources like building permits and interest income are declining or unreliable, though parking meter revenue provides a small bright spot.

Strategic Response

The plan serves as the foundation for a potential operating override request in May 2026. Without an override to increase tax capacity, town leadership warns of deep service cuts, including the potential elimination of approximately 20 town positions and significant staffing reductions within the school district.
