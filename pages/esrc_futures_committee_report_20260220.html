E&RSC Futures Committee report (February 13, 2026)

The Futures Subcommittee report focuses on economic development strategy.

A. Core Thesis

New development is the primary long-term lever to:

  • Generate significant new tax revenue.
  • Reduce reliance on operating overrides.
  • Diversify revenue beyond residential property taxes.

Without new net development revenue, Brookline is likely to require larger and more frequent overrides.

B. Structural Problems Identified

  • Most commercial districts are capped at low FAR, which limits growth potential.
  • Large projects often require zoning amendments, overlay districts, special permits, and two-thirds Town Meeting votes.
  • The process for major projects can take about two years.
  • Current zoning reforms are effectively frozen pending completion of the Comprehensive Plan (expected in 2026).
  • Brookline's development approach is often reactive rather than proactive.

C. Key Recommendations

1. Governance & Leadership

  • Designate a Select Board member to lead economic development.
  • Elevate the economic development function to report to the Town Administrator.
  • Improve coordination among the Planning Board, EDAB, ZBA, and related bodies.
  • Engage proactively with major property owners.

2. Zoning Reform

  • Use the Comprehensive Plan to pre-identify areas for increased density.
  • Implement systematic zoning updates immediately after Plan adoption.
  • Allow the Planning Board to grant certain special permits without ZBA referral.

3. Development Priorities

Major long-term revenue opportunities include:

  • Chestnut Hill Commercial Area (CHCA), estimated at about $5.6M in annual net new taxes at stabilization, comparable in size to the current override request.
  • Waldo-Durgin site redevelopment.
  • Center Street lots redevelopment.
  • Harvard Street mixed-use parcels (Stop & Shop and TJ Maxx).
  • Route 9 corridor.
  • Beacon Street commercial centers.

Hotels are identified as the highest net-revenue use per square foot due to room tax, meals tax, lower service costs, and no school impact.

4. Near-Term Revenue & Efficiency Steps

  • Expand personal property audits (already producing about $200k in additional revenue).
  • Streamline permitting and business licensing.
  • Encourage PILOT participation from nonprofits.
  • Protect commercial district infrastructure (parking, lighting, signage).
