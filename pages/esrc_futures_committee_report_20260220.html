ESRC Futures Committee report (February 13, 2026)

The Futures Subcommittee report focuses on economic development strategy.

A. Core Thesis

New development is the primary long-term lever to:

  • Generate significant new tax revenue.
  • Reduce reliance on operating overrides.
  • Diversify revenue beyond residential property taxes.

Without new net development revenue, Brookline is likely to require larger and more frequent overrides.

B. Structural Problems Identified

  1. Zoning is a significant barrier:
    • Most commercial districts capped at low FAR.
    • Large projects require:
    • Zoning amendments.
    • Overlay districts.
    • Special permits.
    • 2/3 Town Meeting votes.
    • Process can take about 2 years.
  2. Comprehensive Plan delays:
    • Current zoning reforms effectively frozen pending plan completion (expected 2026).
  3. Reactive rather than proactive development culture.

C. Key Recommendations

Governance & Leadership

  • Designate a Select Board member to lead economic development.
  • Elevate economic development function to report to Town Administrator.
  • Improve coordination among Planning Board, EDAB, ZBA, etc.
  • Proactive engagement with major property owners.

Zoning Reform

  • Use Comprehensive Plan to pre-identify areas for increased density.
  • Implement systematic zoning updates immediately after Plan adoption.
  • Allow Planning Board to grant certain special permits without ZBA referral.

Development Priorities

Major long-term revenue opportunities:

  • Chestnut Hill Commercial Area (CHCA).
  • Estimated about $5.6M annual net new taxes at stabilization.
  • Comparable in size to current override request.
  • Waldo-Durgin site.
  • Center Street lots redevelopment.
  • Harvard Street mixed-use parcels (Stop & Shop, TJ Maxx).
  • Route 9 corridor.
  • Beacon Street commercial centers.

Hotels are identified as the highest net revenue use per square foot due to room tax, meals tax, lower service costs, and no school impact.

Near-Term Revenue & Efficiency Steps

  • Expand personal property audits (already producing about $200k additional revenue).
  • Streamline permitting and business licensing.
  • Encourage PILOT participation from nonprofits.
  • Protect commercial district infrastructure (parking, lighting, signage).
