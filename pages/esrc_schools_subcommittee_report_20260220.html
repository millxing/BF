E&RSC Schools Subcommittee report (February 9, 2026)

Two major financial findings areas are discussed: Unreimbursed Special Education for Non-Resident Students and Teacher Salaries.

Unreimbursed Special Education for Non-Resident Students:

  • 144 non-resident students receive in-district special education services.
  • Estimated direct in-district cost per student (FY23-24):
  • ~$30,130 including transportation.
  • ~$27,972 excluding transportation.
  • Applying $27,972 per student to 144 students = $4 million annually in unreimbursed costs.
  • Brookline receives METCO grant funding (~$8,000 per student), but this largely covers METCO-specific staffing and transportation, not general educational programming.
  • No reimbursement for in-district special education from Boston or other sending districts.

Additional concern:

  • Non-resident students have a higher special education participation rate than resident students.
  • Brookline receives below-average METCO reimbursement compared to peer communities.

Recommendation direction:

  • Collaborate with Newton and Lexington to advocate for increased state funding.
  • Reassess long-term sustainability of subsidizing sending districts.

Teacher Salaries

The salary report (pp. 14-18) highlights:

  • Roughly half of Brookline teachers receive annual raises above the contractual 2.75% COLA due to step progression.
  • Actual annual increases range from 5.3% to 8.0% depending on position and movement on the salary scale.
  • Brookline teacher salaries exceed those of comparable districts (Boston, Cambridge, etc.) at most steps, in some cases by $1,000-$5,000+.
  • Healthcare contributions are relatively generous (83% employer contribution for PPO/HMO plans).
  • Brookline high school teaching loads are lower than many peer districts.

Conclusion of the report:

  • Real salary growth (steps + lanes + COLA) outpaces inflation and revenue growth.
  • Long-term sustainability requires alignment of compensation growth with realistic revenue projections, especially absent recurring overrides.
